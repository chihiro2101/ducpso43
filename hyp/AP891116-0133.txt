Employees at a jewelry store huddled in the vault while the shop came down around them
A man in an apartment building stayed in his bathtub while the bathroom walls crumbled and fell
Those were among the stories people in this northern Alabama city swapped on Thursday as they assessed the damage from a tornado that whipped through the heart of the city in a flash leaving at least 17 people dead and hundreds homeless
``Everybody was screaming and windows started breaking and the roof started to fall'' recalled Vincent Davis a fourth-grader at Jones Valley Elementary School
Vincent was staying late at school Wednesday when the twister hit shortly after 430 pm
He was taken to a hospital with minor injuries five of about 30 children in a kindergarten class also were reported hurt
Lynn Culpepper was in his bathtub at the Waterford Square apartments where at least a dozen buildings were destroyed
``The next thing I knew glass was breaking and I was rolling on the ground'' he recalled
Manager Bob Husman of a Golbro jewelry store ordered employees into the vault shortly before the roof and walls crashed down on him 20 feet from the vault
For Ed Rolan a medic the devastation brought back memories of Vietnam
