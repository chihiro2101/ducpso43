SQUADS of workers fanned out across storm-battered Louisiana yesterday to begin a massive rebuilding effort after Hurricane Andrew had flattened whole districts killing two people and injuring dozens more agencies report from Florida and New Orleans
Federal emergency officials acknowledged distribution problems Transportation Secretary Andrew Card yesterday promised 'dramatic' improvements within 24 hours and President George Bush last night ordered troops to Florida without specifying a number
The government estimated it would cost Dollars 20bn-Dollars 30bn to tidy and rebuild in Florida and to care for residents displaced by the storm
The Federal Emergency Management Agency said it was setting aside Dollars 77m to help Louisiana recover
